📺 Is OpenAI scaling back direct checkout inside ChatGPT?
---

In March 2026, The Information reported that OpenAI may be reducing its near-term ambition for fully integrated, first-party checkout inside ChatGPT.

- The report says OpenAI explored direct in-chat checkout, including merchant integrations and potential transaction-fee models.
- It also says the company is now leaning more toward lower-friction commerce paths (for example, referral and affiliate-style flows) instead of acting as the checkout surface itself.
- This appears to be a sequencing decision, not necessarily a permanent cancellation.

Why this matters for the shopping landscape:

- It suggests ChatGPT commerce may evolve in phases:
  - discovery and recommendation first;
  - direct transaction capture later, if user trust, UX, and partner alignment hold.
- It keeps incumbent checkout systems (merchant sites, PSPs, wallet providers) in the critical path for now.

Related context:

- OpenAI has already tested shopping experiences in ChatGPT results, including product cards and merchant links.
- The Information's reporting indicates internal debate around trust, incentives, and how commercial intent should appear in assistant responses.
