In 2013, the Wall Street Journal (a U.S.-based news media) published the following video, titled *"Government's Secret Surveillance Program Explained - "The Short Answer"*.

- The video is about PRISM, a United States government's secret surveillance program that allows the U.S. National Security Agency (NSA) to access phone records and the data of internet companies like Microsoft, Google, and Apple to search for foreign terrorists. 
- The program has been used to prevent terrorist attacks, but it also raises privacy concerns.
